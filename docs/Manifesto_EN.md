# Manifesto: Open Data as Infrastructure

Open data is not a trend. It is infrastructure.

It is the difference between an economy where only the largest actors can see what is happening, and an economy where researchers, small businesses, students, journalists, developers, and public institutions work from a shared reality. When data is open, competition levels out, innovation accelerates, and opportunities are no longer reserved for those who can afford closed databases.

Central.Enterprises exists because we believe that company data must be accessible, verifiable, and useful at scale.

We are building a world where business information is not trapped behind walls, outdated PDFs, or proprietary intermediaries. A world where markets, supply chains, local economies, digital maturity, and competitive landscapes can be understood without needing a corporate contract that excludes 99% of people. A world where a startup can analyze a country with the same level of structure as a multinational, where a student researches with real data, and where a public project is validated with transparent and repeatable evidence.

This isn't about "giving things away." It's about building a standard.

Open data works when it is rigorous. When it is maintained. When it is documented. When it is structured. When it respects privacy and the law. When it is published out of responsibility and not out of exploitation.

Central.Enterprises aims to become the global reference standard for open company data, because the world needs a neutral layer of business reality: a common foundation upon which anyone can build.

A layer that is:

* Precise enough to be trusted
* Recent enough to be acted upon
* Structured enough to be processed
* Accessible enough to be universal
* Responsible enough to last

The internet is full of public signals: official websites, public listings, contact pages, descriptions, hours, aggregated reviews, categories, and digital footprints that businesses themselves publish to be found. We come from a builders' mindset: engineering, publishing, teaching, and operating real digital products. Systems that work under real constraints (budget, time, legal limits, quality thresholds) and with a constant reality: the world changes every day.

We don't idealize data. We are obsessed with it being useful.

We believe that the future of business intelligence is not another closed database with another contract. The future is an open ecosystem where the data layer is shared, and differentiation happens in tools, analysis, services, integrations, and creativity.

Central.Enterprises is building that shared layer.

## Why open data, why now

Because the world moves too fast for closed systems.

Markets change weekly. Sectors shift month by month. Local economies evolve daily. Closures, openings, rebrandings, website migrations, and platform changes happen constantly. If data is locked away, it ages and dies. If data is open, it is tested, reused, improved, referenced, and questioned. Openness creates accountability. Accountability creates quality. Quality creates trust.

Open data also creates a multiplier effect:

* A dataset can turn into hundreds or thousands of projects.
* A publication can trigger local solutions.
* A clear structure can become a standard adopted by others.

When the standard improves, everyone wins.

## What we mean by "Open Company Data"

Open company data is not personal data. It is not about individuals or sensitive attributes. It is about companies and their public presence: the information an organization publishes to be found and that customers use to decide.

Our principle is simple: respect for privacy, respect for the law, focus on companies, and total transparency.

That means:

* We work with company-level information.
* We prioritize public signals and sources with reuse rights, and we explain it in each dataset.
* We design datasets for research and legitimate commercial use.
* We do not build invasive personal profiling.
* We document so that the data is reproducible.
* We treat quality as an engineering discipline, not a marketing promise.

And something important: if a company requests a correction or removal of information, we apply an agile and verifiable rectification mechanism. Openness without responsibility is not openness: it is noise.

## What we publish and why it matters

We don't believe in vague datasets. We believe in practical datasets: structured records that reflect the real footprint of companies and that can be filtered, deduplicated, enriched, and analyzed.

A modern business dataset must represent what a business is today in the digital economy: an entity with location, category, aggregated reputation, contact surfaces, and changing web presence.

Typical fields (company, public signals, ready for analysis):

* Name and categories
* Aggregated rating and number of reviews
* Hours and public description
* Address, locality, province/administrative area, postal code, country
* Website and public profile links (where available)
* Published corporate phone number
* Coordinates and map references
* Technical metadata (capture date, version/snapshot, origin, license, quality)

Regarding content that is sensitive by nature (e.g., full review texts or emails): it will only be published when there is a clear fit with legality, reuse rights, and responsibility. The rule is to prioritize utility without crossing lines.

### Geographic data: the backbone of business analysis

There is no business intelligence without geography. Postal codes, administrative divisions, and locality mapping are the backbone of any regional analysis: segmentation, planning, resource allocation, trend mapping, economic studies.

That is why Central.Enterprises also publishes open and rigorous geographic layers as part of the ecosystem. Not as an accessory, but as a pillar.

## The publishing philosophy: "truth in snapshot form"

One of the most honest things in data is saying what you are offering.

Central.Enterprises publishes reliable snapshots of reality at a given time. This is valuable for comparing, researching, and deciding.

We don't aim to "hand-polish" every record: it would be inaccessible, expensive, and slow. The world doesn't need a perfect dataset of 10 companies. It needs a solid dataset of millions.

That is why we design for:

* Scale
* Consistency
* Repeatability
* Continuous improvement

Each dataset is published with a clear snapshot identity, release date, documentation, and, where applicable, changes from previous versions.

## How we build trust

Trust requires discipline.

Open data without discipline becomes noise. And when noise wins, the mission dies.

Central.Enterprises builds trust with engineering and publishing:

* Consistency across regions: same columns, same formats, same logic
* Deduplication: stable analysis, less distortion
* Geographic coherence: ZIP codes, provinces, and localities aligned
* Link integrity: verified websites, checkable references
* Freshness and versioning: snapshots and clear cadence
* Documentation: field dictionary, limits, examples, license, and origin
* Responsible limits: focus on the company, respect for privacy, removal/correction when appropriate

Furthermore, we believe in transparently separating mission and business: an open foundation that empowers everyone, and premium layers (tools, integrations, enrichments, automations, audits) for operational needs. A hybrid model can coexist without betraying openness if the open part remains useful, maintained, and reliable.
